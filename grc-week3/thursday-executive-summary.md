## Executive Summary

This report is able to provide a clear and executive overview of the current cybersecurity, as well as the compliance posture of Sohail Smart Solutions' EdTech platform. It is able to summarize the most significant risks that were identified during the recent Governance, Risk, and Compliance (GRC) assessment and presents them in a way that is able to support the  business's decision-making process. Rather than focusing only on the technical details, this report is able to highlight the areas that require management attention, and it is also able to explain the potential business impact, as well as even recommend the practical actions that will strengthen the organization's overall security posture.

The assessment is based on the control review, which was completed earlier this week, which had included the mapping of security findings to NIST 800-53 controls and the qualitative risk assessment developed using a likelihood × impact methodology. Overall, the organization is able to demonstrate a reasonable security foundation, but there are several control gaps that require timely remediation to reduce operational, financial, and compliance risk.

---

## Overall Risk Posture

The overall cybersecurity risk posture of Sohail Smart Solutions is assessed as **Moderate to High**. The review was able to showcase that the organization has implemented several foundational security practices, which include both the structured governance processes and even the core security controls. However, a number of control weaknesses do remain that could potentially be able to expose the business to operational disruption and even unauthorized access, as well as compliance concerns if left unresolved.

The most significant risks identified during the assessment relate to user authentication, access management, security monitoring, backup readiness, and protection of sensitive information. While none of these issues are able to indicate an immediate system failure, together they are able to increase the organization's exposure to cyberattacks and should also be treated as priority improvement areas.

---

## Top 3 Business Priorities

### 1. Strengthen Identity and Access Management

The highest priority is improving the identity and access management by implementing Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) and even enforcing the Principle of Least Privilege across all of the user accounts. These measures will significantly be able to reduce the risk of unauthorized access and even protect sensitive student and employee, as well as the organizational data.

### 2. Improve Security Monitoring and Incident Visibility

Security logging and monitoring should be enhanced to be able to ensure that the important events are recorded and reviewed, as well as retained. Stronger audit capabilities will help the organization to be able to detect suspicious activity earlier, as well as support the incident investigations, and even demonstrate compliance with the recognized security frameworks.

### 3. Increase Operational Resilience

The organization should strengthen its backup and recovery processes by regularly testing the restoration procedures and even maintaining the secure backup copies. Improving the resilience will reduce the downtime, as well as help minimize the impact of cybersecurity incidents, and even help maintain the business continuity.

## Key Compliance Gaps

The assessment identified a number of compliance gaps that need to be filled to be able to improve the overall security posture of the organization.

| Compliance Gap | Related Framework |
|----------------|-------------------|
| The Multi-Factor Authentication is not consistently enforced, as well as implemented for the user accounts. | NIST 800-53 IA-2, IA-5 / ISO 27001 Annex A - Identity and Access Management |
| The Excessive user privileges are able to increase the risk of the unauthorized access to confidential and sensitive information. | NIST 800-53 AC-2, AC-3, AC-6 / ISO 27001 Annex A - Access Control |
| Security logging and audit monitoring tend to require further improvement and enhancement. | NIST 800-53 AU-2, AU-6, AU-12 / ISO 27001 Annex A - Logging and Monitoring |
| Backup validation and recovery testing are not performed regularly. | ISO 27001 Annex A - Information Backup and Business Continuity |
| Sensitive information tends to require stronger protection during storage and transmission. | NIST 800-53 SC-8, SC-28 / ISO 27001 Annex A - Cryptography and Data Protection |

---

## Recommended Next Steps

The current evaluation suggests and recommends the following new actions for Sohail Smart Solutions:

1. Ensure that the Multi-Factor Authentication is enforced, as well as implemented for all the privileged and user accounts.
2. Make sure to conduct a thorough analysis and review of the users’ permissions and also enforce and implement the Role-Based Access Control (RBAC) using the Principle of Least Privilege.
3. Strengthen the audit logging, along with the monitoring, and even the regular log review processes.
4. Establish a routine vulnerability management, as well as the security patching process.
5. Make sure to conduct regular backup restoration tests, as well as update the business continuity procedures at all times.
6. Regularly perform periodic compliance reviews against the NIST 800-53 and ISO/IEC 27001 to be able to verify that the implemented controls remain effective.
